How to use the Egle Motors system

A practical guide to every app in the group — what each one is for, who works in it, and the exact steps for the jobs you do every day.

Start here

Signing in, and how the apps fit together

You have one login for everything. Sign in once at the hub and every app you're allowed to use opens without asking again.

Your one login

Go to agcmotors.com and sign in with your work email and password. You land on Your apps — a grid of tiles. Click a tile to open that app; you're already signed in when you arrive.

If a tile is missing

You only see the apps you've been granted. A missing tile means missing access, not a broken app — ask an administrator to add it in the Staff app. Tasks is the one app everybody gets and it can't be removed.

Five wrong attempts locks you out

After five failed sign-ins your account locks for 15 minutes. Waiting is the only cure — or an administrator can reset your password, which clears the lock. A successful sign-in resets the counter.

Log Out signs you out of every app at once, not just the one you're looking at. On a shared machine, always use it rather than closing the tab.

Updating an exchange rate

  1. Sidebar → Exchange Rates.
  2. In the US Dollar section, type the new figures against GHS, GBP or EUR. Blank rows are left alone. Press Save rates.
  3. In the Ghana Cedi section, choose the currency, enter GHS per 1 unit and an Effective date, then Add rate.
  4. Check the Current rate per currency cards show what you expect.

Rates are dated, not overwritten. The current rate is the newest one dated today or earlier, and each payment freezes the rate it used — so history never shifts under you.

Access denied gives no explanation

If you follow a direct link into an app you don't have access to, you're bounced back to Your apps with no message telling you why. If a link seems to do nothing, that's what happened — ask for access rather than retrying.

02 · Selling a vehicle

Sales

From first enquiry to handing over the keys. Every vehicle sale is built here, and every credit agreement in the business starts here.

Who uses it
Salespeople, Commercial Manager, Receivables, Compliance, PDI
Currency
Ghana Cedi (GH₵)
Reports
Administrators only
Status
Live

The eight stages

Every deal walks the same path. Each stage belongs to one department — you can only act when the deal is at your stage; otherwise you see “Waiting on <department>”.

#StageOwned byTarget
1OpportunitySales3 days
2Vehicle SelectionPDI2 days
3Pre-Delivery InspectionPDI2 days
4Insurance & DVLACompliance3 days
5ContractCommercial Manager2 days

7Entries & InvoiceReceivables2 days
8Vehicle ReleasePDI + Sales2 days

Selling a vehicle, start to finish

  1. Log the lead. + New Lead → first name, last name and an assigned salesperson are required. Log every walk-in, whether or not they buy.
  2. Quote it (optional). Quotations → New quotation → price, deposit, term, rate. Present it, then convert — the opportunity arrives pre-filled. Remember the quote is financials only: it excludes insurance, DVLA and extras.
  3. Open the opportunity from the lead, then fill the Opportunity stage: agreement type (CSA or WNP), vehicle, deposit, term, frequency, first repayment date. Watch the deposit warning — below the policy minimum it says “— below policy”. Check the Affordability badge.
  4. Vehicle Selection. PDI allocates a physical vehicle from stock.
  5. Pre-Delivery Inspection. The checklist is done in the Service app; the result appears here as a pass/fail card. Nothing to type.
  6. Insurance & DVLA. Compliance enters insurer, policy number, cover and the registration number.
  7. Contract. The Commercial Manager finalises terms and completes the legal fields — buyer details, and the guarantor block if Include a guarantor is ticked. Then Generate agreement and use the Deal jacket to upload the Ghana Card and capture the customer's signature.
  8. Contract Executed → execution date and who was present.
  9. Entries & Invoice. Receivables records the deposit and payment date. The repayment schedule and the purchase reference are generated here.
  10. Vehicle Release. Release date plus a handover photo, then Complete & release. The lead becomes a customer.
Two hard blocks on release

The vehicle cannot be released until the first payment is recorded and the full deposit is collected — deposits payable in parts must be complete. The app will not let you past this, and it is not a bug.

Other things you'll do here

  • Desking — build and compare deal scenarios side by side (CSA vs WNP, different terms and deposits), then Accept the one the customer wants.
  • Trade-in — appraise a part-exchange, send the allowance for approval, then accept it into used inventory.
  • F&I — finance reserve, add-on products and deposits. Deposits already held can be applied, refunded or forfeited.
  • Block a lead — Receivables can block a customer with an outstanding balance, which stops any new opportunity until they're unblocked.
  • Mark lost — with a reason. This frees the reserved vehicle back into stock.
Sales glossary
Lead → Opportunity → Customer
A lead is any enquiry. An opportunity is an active deal. A customer is a lead who completed a purchase.
CSA — Credit Sale Agreement
The customer buys on credit and owns the vehicle, paying in installments. Terms of 6, 12 or 24 months; weekly or monthly.
WNP — Work and Pay
The customer works with the vehicle and pays from earnings; ownership transfers at the end. Terms up to 60 months, weekly only.
Affordability
Affordable / Caution / High risk, based on the installment as a share of monthly income — caution at 30%, high risk above 45%.
Front gross / Back gross
Front is the margin on the vehicle itself. Back is finance reserve plus product margin.
Deal jacket
The permanent folder of contract, ID documents and signature. Signed documents can't be altered afterwards.
SLA / aging
The target days for each stage. Deals show On track, Due or Over SLA.
Reference formats
Lead MA-202607-001 · Opportunity OPP-2026-014 · Agreement CSA-MA-202607-014.

Pricing

The single place selling prices are set — labour rates and part prices. Sales, Service and Parts all read from here automatically.

Who uses it
Everyone can look up; admins set prices
Currency
Ghana Cedi (GH₵)
Read-only users
Marked “· read-only” in the sidebar
Status
Live
Nothing is ever overwritten

Setting a new price doesn't replace the old one — it closes the old row with an end date and opens a new one. That's why every history table reads “01 Jan 2026 – present” and the live row is tagged current. You can also date a change forward.

Changing one part's price

  1. Part Prices → search by name, SKU or OEM number.
  2. Click Set / history.
  3. Choose the price type — retail, workshop or trade — enter the price and an effective date, then Set price.

Changing many prices at once

  1. Bulk Update → ↓ Download CSV template.
  2. Fill it in Excel, then save as CSV — not .xlsx. The app will reject a spreadsheet file.
  3. Upload → Validate. Bad rows are listed with the reason.
  4. Commit N valid rows. Invalid rows are skipped; the good ones still go through.

Importing a container of stock

This is the longest job in the app, and the one that produces landed costs.

  1. Price Lists → New price list → name it, choose the kind, set an effective date → Create draft list.
  2. Open the list → Import CSV.
  3. Work the four steps: Upload → Map columns → Validate → Apply. On the mapping step, tick the container-totals box if your freight and duty figures are for the whole shipment — the app then spreads them across units pro-rata. Leave it unticked if they're already per-unit.
  4. Review the validation report. Fix problems in the sheet and retry if needed.
  5. Spot-check the items, correcting any row with Edit. Landed cost recalculates itself.
  6. Publish list and confirm. Prices go live to Sales, Service and Parts, and a published list becomes read-only.
Two imports, two different behaviours

The price list import is all-or-nothing: if any row fails, nothing is written. The Bulk Update import is not: it skips bad rows and commits the rest. Know which one you're using.

04 · Selling a vehicle

PDI — Pre-Delivery Inspection

Two checks: when a vehicle arrives from a supplier, and before it goes to a customer. A passed arrival check is what makes a vehicle sellable.

Who uses it
Inspectors; supervisors maintain checklists
Permissions
Everyone with access can do everything
Device
Desktop or landscape tablet — not phones
Status
Live

Arrival inspection

  1. Vehicles appear in the Arrival queue once Purchasing receives them against a PO.
  2. Click Inspect. Work the checklist, marking each item Pass, Fail or N/A. A Fail reveals a note box — describe the defect.
  3. Record the odometer and fuel level, and add photos if uploads are switched on.
  4. Mark passed — the vehicle becomes sellable stock and leaves the queue. Or Mark failed — it stays in the queue for re-inspection.

Pre-delivery inspection

Same form, reached from the PDI queue once Sales creates a delivery. Passing writes the result back to the delivery so Sales can submit it for approval; failing leaves Sales blocked until you re-inspect.

Every item defaults to Pass

The form opens with everything already marked Pass, defect notes are optional, and an inspection with failed items can still be marked passed. The software enforces nothing. The quality of the record depends entirely on the inspector actually working through each item — this is the single biggest thing to cover in training and supervision.

No “saved” confirmation

After you submit, you land on the vehicle's history page with no success banner. Check that your new inspection card is at the top of the list — that's your confirmation.

Supervisors: checklists live under Templates, one active template per kind. Ticking a second one active gives no warning but only one will be used — verify there is exactly one active arrival template and one active PDI template before rollout.

05 · Getting paid

Cashier

Recording money in, issuing receipts, and balancing the drawer at the end of the day.

Who uses it
Cashiers; managers approve and post
Default currency
GHS (USD is the base)
Manager-only
Void, cash-up approval, Posting Bridge
Status
Live

Recording a payment

  1. + New Receipt.
  2. Search the customer by name, phone or email and select them. A walk-in with no record? Leave it empty and use source Other with a note.
  3. Choose what the money is for, then pick the specific sale, invoice or counter sale.
  4. Enter the amount — or click the Fill GHS … shortcut to use the outstanding balance.
  5. Set currency and method.
  6. Enter the reference. This is required for Mobile Money, bank transfer and cheque — the MoMo transaction ID, cheque number or transfer reference.
  7. Record Receipt. You land on the receipt, where Print / PDF and WhatsApp give the customer their copy.
Installments go elsewhere for now

Choosing Installment shows a notice and disables the button. Record installment payments in the Sales app until the credit allocation function ships.

End-of-day cash-up

  1. At the start of the day: Cash-Up → enter the opening float → Open session. You can only have one open at a time.
  2. At close: Count & close →. Enter what you physically counted per method and currency.
  3. Add a note explaining any difference, then Close session. The difference between counted and system is your variance — zero shows green, anything else red.
  4. A manager opens the closed session and presses Approve (manager sign-off).

Only the cashier who opened a session can close it — not even a manager can do it for you.

Voiding a mistake (managers)

Open the receipt → Void receipt… → type a reason (required) → Confirm void. This creates a reversing entry; the original stays on record with a VOIDED badge. Nothing is ever deleted.

Reconciling electronic payments

When the MoMo or bank statement arrives: Reconcile → set the statement date → tick every receipt that appears on it → Mark selected reconciled. Cash receipts never appear here.

06 · Getting paid

Collections

The working app for chasing overdue customers. Build call lists, log every contact, record promises, apply fees, and take a case through to settlement, restructure or repossession.

Who uses it
Collectors and their supervisors
Permissions
Flat — everyone can do everything
Sees
The whole book, regardless of assignment
Status
Live
This is the app to work accounts in

Collections and Receivables look almost identical and share most screens. Collections is the fuller one — it alone has call lists, promise follow-ups, charges and restructure. Unless you have a specific reason, work here. See the note under Receivables.

The daily collections run

  1. Dashboard — read the arrears aging tiles (1-29 / 30-59 / 60-89 / 90+) and the arrears trend. Filter by collector if you supervise several.
  2. Supervisor: Worklists → create a named list, assign a collector, open it, then Build list to pull in the worst accounts from arrears.
  3. Collector: open your list and press Work on the first account, then click the customer to open their page.
  4. Read the repayment schedule and the History — see what's already been tried before you call.
  5. Make contact, then fill Log collection action: Call, SMS, Visit, Letter or Field agent. Add the outcome in plain words (“no answer”, “will pay Friday”).
  6. If they commit to paying, choose Promise to pay — the promised amount and date are both required — so it lands in the follow-up queue.
  7. Back on the list, mark the item Complete or Skip.

Promises

A logged promise appears in Follow-ups as upcoming, becomes due today, then broken if unpaid. Close it with Kept or Broken — the outcome is written back to the account history.

Fees, settlement and restructure

  • Charges — apply a late fee or other charge with a reason. It feeds the settlement quote. Waive removes it for goodwill or error.
  • Early settlement — the quote is overdue + future + charges − rebate − credit balance. Read it to the customer, take the money, then enter the amount received (it must cover the quote), pick the method, tick the confirmation box and Post settlement. This creates the receipt and closes the agreement in one step.
  • Restructure — re-terms the remaining principal onto a brand-new agreement and closes the current one. Requires new term, rate, frequency, interest method and start date.

Repossession

  1. On the customer's page, write the reason and evidence of collection attempts (required), tick the confirmation, and press Flag for repossession.
  2. In the Repossessions queue, advance the case one stage at a time: Issue notice → Mark recovered → Move to yard (enter the yard location) → then either Return to customer or Write off.
Mobile money prompts do not reach the customer

The Request pay button and the Mobile Money desk mark a request as “prompted” without calling any provider — the customer's phone does not buzz. This is waiting on aggregator sign-off. Do not tell a customer to expect a prompt, and do not treat a sent request as a payment attempt.

A flagged case now waits for sign-off

Flagging no longer makes a case actionable. The row shows Awaiting GM approval and there is no Issue notice button until a General Manager signs off in the CSA & WNP app. This is enforced by the system, not by procedure — the notice cannot be issued without the approval, however the request is made.

Collections glossary
DPD — Days Past Due
How many days the oldest unpaid amount is late. Drives the bucket.
Bucket
The lateness band: current, 1-29, 30-59, 60-89, 90+. Colour-coded green through dark red.
PAR 30 / 60 / 90
Portfolio At Risk — the share of the book more than 30, 60 or 90 days late. The core health measure.
Overdue vs Outstanding
Overdue is the part already late. Outstanding is everything still owed.
PTP — Promise to Pay
A commitment to a specific amount on a specific date, tracked for follow-up.
Credit balance
Customer money on file not yet applied to an installment. It reduces a settlement quote.
Rebate
The interest discount for settling early — a share of interest not yet earned.
Snapshot
The amount and bucket frozen when an account was added to a call list, so the list doesn't shift while you work it.

08 · Getting paid

CSA & WNP

A read-only window on the credit book, plus one job that happens nowhere else: a manager approving or rejecting a repossession.

Who uses it
Anyone reviewing credit; managers for sign-off
You can change
Only a repossession decision
Everything else
Read-only
Status
Live
Set expectations before anyone opens this

You cannot create a deal, take a payment, log a collection action, record a promise or settle an agreement here. Deals are created in Sales, money is receipted in Cashier, and accounts are worked in Collections. Staff who aren't told this will hunt for buttons that don't exist.

Reviewing a customer

  1. Agreements — accounts in arrears sort to the top automatically. There is no search or filter, so scroll.
  2. Click the customer to open Schedule vs paid — one row per installment showing what was due, what was paid, and the status. Overdue rows are tinted red, partial rows amber.
  3. Watch for the footnote about unpaid charges — fees sit outside the installment rows, which is why the totals can look like they disagree.

Approving a repossession (managers)

  1. A collections officer flags the case in Collections. It appears here with a red flagged badge and their note.
  2. Click the customer's name to review the agreement first.
  3. Back on Repossessions, press Approve or Reject, add a note if useful, then Confirm approval / Confirm rejection.

Approving moves the case to notice issued and records who approved it. There is no undo. The note is optional in the software — if your policy requires a reason, that's a rule people must follow, not one the app enforces.

Rejecting closes the case and frees the agreement, so the same customer can be flagged again later if things deteriorate.

This screen is the only way a notice gets issued

Collections can flag a case but cannot issue the notice. Only a General Manager (or an administrator) can approve, and the decision is stamped with their name. Approvals also appear in the Staff app's Approvals inbox.

Anyone without sign-off rights sees the queue but no buttons, with a notice explaining who can decide.

Service

Who uses it
Advisors, technicians, workshop managers
Technicians can't
Change rates or billed hours
Wall display
Live board, refreshes every 30s
Status
Live

The eleven repair-order statuses

Booked → Checked in → Diagnosed → Quoted → Approved → In progress → Awaiting parts → Completed → Invoiced → Paid → Released. You move one step at a time — no skipping.

A booked customer job

  1. Bookings → Add booking: customer, vehicle, repair type, date and time, bay, technician. Capacity is checked when you save, so a clash comes back as an error.
  2. On the day, open the booking → Check in →. Record mileage, fuel, existing damage, customer items left in the vehicle, condition photos, and the customer's signature on the pad. Then Check in & open repair order — this creates the job card and builds the task checklist.
  3. Inspect, then advance to → Diagnosed.
  4. Add labour lines (search the job type — it fills the description and rate from the rate card) and parts lines.
  5. Snapshot current lines → new quote version, then → Quoted.
  6. Share the quote with Copy link or WhatsApp. The customer taps ✓ Approve this quote on their phone — or you record their verbal approval with Mark approved and how they said yes. Then → Approved.
  7. Take the deposit if one is required.
  8. → In progress. Technicians assign themselves tasks and press Start, Pause and Complete.
  9. Waiting on a part? Raise request to Parts, then → Awaiting parts; come back once it's issued.
  10. → Completed → Generate invoice → Record payment → → Released.
Three gates you cannot talk your way past

A customer job can't start work without an approved quote. A walk-in (customer-owned) vehicle also needs manager approval before work starts. And the vehicle cannot be released until the invoice is paid in full — that one is enforced by the database itself.

Check which clock you have

Clock-on/clock-off on the Workshop jobs screen depends on a database migration that may not be applied. If the Clock column shows a dash and a message about pending columns, type the hours into Actual and save instead. The Task board timers are separate and do work.

Service glossary
RO — Repair Order
The job card, numbered like RO-000123.
Job line vs Task
A job line is a billable labour item. A task is a checklist step a technician clocks against. The app has both, on different screens.
Bill to
Customer, Internal (stock reconditioning), or Warranty.
Internal vs External
Internal is a dealership vehicle. External is a customer-owned walk-in — and needs manager approval before work starts.
Issued
The part has physically left the store for this job and stock has come down. Unissued means planned but not yet taken.
Quote version
A frozen snapshot of the lines. Change the lines, take a new snapshot.
Actual vs Billed hours
What the technician clocked, versus what the customer pays.
Net due
Invoice total minus any deposit already applied.

Parts

The store. Booking stock in, issuing it to jobs, selling over the counter, handling returns, and counting the shelves.

Who uses it
Parts clerks and the storeman
Parts created in
Purchasing — not here
Prices owned by
Pricing — not here
Status
Live
Three numbers people confuse constantly

On hand is what's physically on the shelf. Allocated is on hand but already reserved for a job or order. Available is on hand minus allocated — and it's the only one that matters when you're selling or issuing. It turns red at zero.

Booking stock in against a purchase order

  1. PO Receiving → open the purchase order.
  2. Check every line reads allocated. If any says “not allocated”, stop — landed cost must be allocated in the Purchasing app first, and receiving is blocked until it is.
  3. Enter the quantity received per line (and a VIN per unit for vehicle lines), add a note, then Post receipt.

Issuing parts to a workshop job

Either wait for Service to raise a request — Requests → Issue or Reject — or push it yourself from Issue to RO with the part, the RO/job reference and a quantity. Parts with nothing available are greyed out and can't be selected.

A counter sale

  1. Sale Orders → ＋ New sale order. Enter a customer note (or leave it blank for “Walk-in”), search and add parts, adjust quantity and price.
  2. Create sale order.
  3. If any line is a serialised part, open the sale and Attach a specific serial per unit until each line reads Ready.
  4. Choose the currency and press Fulfil / Pick. Stock is issued, the sale completes and a receipt is recorded.
  5. Invoice → Print / Save PDF for the customer.

Stocktake

  1. Stocktake → Count sheets → Print count sheets. They're grouped by bin.
  2. Count the shelves, then type the counts into the Counted column. Variances calculate as you go, and blank rows are ignored — a partial count is fine.
  3. Post variances. Each one becomes a stock adjustment.
  4. Print the Variance report for the manager.
Large adjustments need a manager

Manual adjustments above a value threshold are blocked with “a manager must approve it”. Admins auto-approve their own. The Approved column on Recent adjustments shows which is which.

Read the confirmation dialogs. Every stock-moving button pops one, and the text spells out exactly what will happen — “Post this receipt? Stock and weighted-average cost will update.” Clicking through them blindly is how stock goes wrong.

11 · Workshop & stock

Purchasing

Suppliers, the parts catalogue, purchase orders, and working out what imported stock actually costs once freight and duty are added.

Who uses it
Buyers and the purchasing manager
Home currency
GHS; suppliers usually foreign
Receiving happens in
Parts — not here
Status
Live

Raising and placing an order

  1. New PO → supplier, order type, currency (the exchange rate fills itself), expected date → Create draft PO.
  2. Add the lines: Add part, Add vehicle, and Add charge for freight, duty, clearing or insurance.
  3. Mark sent once it's with the supplier, then Confirm order when they acknowledge.
  4. Allocate the landed cost. Choose By value or By quantity and press Allocate. The banner must turn green and read “allocated”.
  5. Goods are then received in the Parts app. The PO status updates itself.
  6. Once everything has arrived, Close PO.
Allocation is not optional

Nothing can be received until landed cost is allocated — even on a PO with no charges at all. And anything that changes the numbers wipes the allocation: adding a late freight invoice, changing the currency, changing the exchange rate. Re-allocate before receiving the rest, or the store will be blocked.

Late charges

A freight or duty invoice that arrives after ordering goes on with Add late charge on a confirmed or partially-received PO — then Re-allocate immediately.

Backorders

A short delivery leaves the balance under Backorders. Either Receive it when the rest arrives, or Cancel it with a reason — the reason is mandatory.

What locks and when

Lines can be edited while a PO is draft or sent. Once confirmed, lines lock and only charges can be added. Only a draft can be deleted — everything else is cancelled. And a PO can only be closed from received.

The exchange rate on a PO is locked to the admin rate for most users; only administrators can override it. Rates themselves are set in the Operations Hub.

12 · Running the business

Staff

People and permissions. Adding staff, deciding which apps they can open, approvals, and commission.

Who uses it
Administrators; everyone for their own profile
Grants access to
Every app in the group
Staff are
Deactivated, never deleted
Status
Live
Position is not permission

A person's position (“Workshop Foreman”) is a job title and grants nothing at all. What they can open is decided entirely by their access group. This is the single most misunderstood thing in the system — changing someone's job title does not change what they can see.

Adding someone

  1. Invite staff. Only the login email and a temporary password are strictly required; add name, position, phone, branch and hire date too.
  2. Choose an access group now, or leave it as “No group — no app access” and set it later.
  3. Create staff member.
  4. Give the person their email and temporary password. They complete their own profile — administrators never type someone else's bank details or Ghana Card number.

Changing what someone can open

  1. Access groups → New group → name it, tick the apps it should grant → Create group. Do this once per job function, not once per person.
  2. Roles & access → find the person → set their access group → Save.
  3. Check the ACCESS card on their record lists the apps you expect.

Editing a group's app list updates every member immediately — no per-person rework.

Commission

  1. Set one rule per role: percent of sale price, percent of margin, or a flat amount per unit. Saving a rule for a role that already has one replaces it.
  2. Pick the month and press Generate / refresh. This reads delivered sales only, and it's safe to re-run — sellers with no active rule are reported as “skipped”.
  3. Review the Pending tab, tick the rows and Approve selected. Use void on anything wrong.
  4. Export CSV for the payroll run, then mark the rows paid afterwards.

The system does not pay anyone. “Paid” is a record-keeping marker; payroll happens outside.

Offboarding

Open their record and press Deactivate. This revokes all app access and blocks login immediately. Watch for the warning about open sales and unpaid commission — reassign open sales in the Sales app first. Reactivating is possible, but access must be granted again.

Safety rails

You cannot deactivate your own account, cannot deactivate the last member of the admin group, and cannot move yourself out of the admin group if you're the last admin.

The database migration behind this app may not have been applied. If every screen is empty and the insurer list won't populate, the tables don't exist yet — that's a setup step, not something a user can fix. Check with the development team before running a session on this app.

First: add your insurers

Insurers → + Add insurer. No policy can be saved until at least one exists — the policy form disables its save button and tells you so.

Insuring a vehicle

  1. Vehicles → Add policy (or use the red callout on the dashboard, which links straight from an uninsured vehicle).
  2. Search the vehicle by VIN, registration, make or model and pick it.
  3. Fill the policy: insurer, policy number, cover type, start and end dates are all required. Premium and the document scan are optional.
  4. Save policy. The alert check re-runs immediately.

Filing a vehicle document

Same shape: find the vehicle, choose the type (Roadworthy, DVLA registration, Customs declaration or Other), enter the reference and dates, attach the scan.

No expiry date means no alert, ever

Only the vehicle is genuinely required when saving a document. If you leave the expiry date blank the record saves happily and the system will never warn you about it. Always fill the expiry date.

The daily alerts round

  1. Alerts — the sidebar badge shows how many are open.
  2. Filter to Open, assign an owner with Assign.
  3. Press Ack to show you've seen it and taken it on.
  4. Chase the renewal. Once the new policy or document is filed, press Resolve.

Alerts appear on their own every night — nobody creates them by hand. The same alert narrows its window from 30-day to 14-day to 7-day as the date approaches, rather than piling up new ones.

Uninsured credit vehicles

The dashboard shows a red count of vehicles on live credit agreements with no comprehensive cover. Company rule: credit vehicles must be insured, so this number should be zero. Third-party cover does not clear it.

Raising a task

  1. New Task. Only the title is required.
  2. Optionally assign it, set a priority and a due date.
  3. To attach it to a real record, choose the type — customer, credit agreement, sale, repair order, purchase order or vehicle — then search (type at least two characters) and click the match.
  4. Add notes; they post as the first comment on the task.
  5. Create task.

Working your list

My Tasks groups everything assigned to you into Overdue, Due today, Upcoming and No due date. Open a task and press Start, then Complete — or click the circle on any row to jump straight to done. Post progress as comments; commenting automatically makes you a watcher.

A linked task shows Open ↗, which jumps to that record in whichever app owns it. That's the quickest way to get from “chase this customer” to the customer's actual account.

One editing quirk

The Edit screen lets you change the linked record, but that change isn't saved — only title, description, assignee, due date and priority are. If you attached a task to the wrong record, create a new task rather than trying to re-link it.

Known quirks

Things that will confuse people during training. These are software issues, not user error — tell staff so they don't waste time.

Menu items you can't use

Admin-only sections no longer appear for staff who can't open them. If you don't see Posting Bridge in Cashier or Commissions in Staff, that's correct — they are for managers and administrators. And if you follow a link into an app you don't have, the hub now names the app you were refused instead of quietly returning you to the tile grid.

“No access” screens

Each app names itself and gives you the actual remedy: ask an administrator to add this app to your access group. Access always comes from your access group in the Staff app — never from your job title.

Tasks is the exception worth knowing. Everyone has Tasks, so being refused there usually means your login isn't linked to an active staff record, or you tried to open the admin-only Team Board.

Features that look live but aren't

  • Simulated Mobile money prompts (Collections). The request is recorded but no provider is called — the customer's phone does not ring. Don't tell a customer to expect a prompt, and don't treat a sent request as an attempt to pay.
  • Conditional Photo and signature uploads (PDI, Compliance, Service check-in). If storage isn't configured the field is disabled and says so.
  • Conditional Clock-on / clock-off (Service). May be pending a database migration; enter hours manually if so.

Demo data

The CSA & WNP dashboard may still show a yellow “Seed data — demo book, not real figures” banner. Confirm whether the live cutover has happened before showing that screen to staff, or they'll take the numbers seriously.

Group glossary

Terms that mean the same thing everywhere in the system.

Contracts and credit

CSA — Credit Sale Agreement
Customer buys on credit and owns the vehicle, paying in installments.
WNP — Work and Pay
Customer works with the vehicle and pays from earnings; ownership transfers at the end. Weekly payments only.
Installment plan
A saved template of deposit, term, frequency and rate, offered when creating an agreement.
Grace days
Days past due before a late fee applies.
Settlement rebate
The share of not-yet-earned interest handed back when a customer pays off early.

Money

GH₵ / GHS
Ghana Cedi. The currency customers pay in. USD is the system's base currency for internal totals.
Landed cost
What an imported item really costs: supplier price plus freight, duty and clearing, per unit.
FOB
The supplier's price before shipping.
WAC — weighted average cost
The running average unit cost of stock, recalculated each time a receipt is posted.
Front / Back gross
Margin on the vehicle itself, versus margin from finance and add-on products.

Arrears

DPD
Days Past Due — how late the oldest unpaid amount is.
Bucket
The lateness band: current, 1-29, 30-59, 60-89, 90+.
PAR 30 / 60 / 90
Portfolio At Risk — the share of the book more than 30, 60 or 90 days late.
PTP
Promise to Pay — a commitment to an amount on a date.

Operations

PO — Purchase Order
An order placed on a supplier.
GRN — Goods Received Note
The record of stock arriving.
RO — Repair Order
A workshop job card.
PDI
Pre-Delivery Inspection — the check before a customer takes the vehicle.
DVLA
The vehicle licensing authority; the DVLA registration number is the number plate.
KYC
Know Your Customer — the identity documents held against a customer, chiefly the Ghana Card.

Access

Access group
The named bundle of apps a person can open. The only thing that grants access.
Position
A job title. Displayed everywhere, grants nothing.
SSO
Single sign-on. One login at the hub opens every app you're allowed to use.
